Our Classes

Group music lessons for primary and intermediate school-aged children, every Saturday morning at Dyer Street School.

🎼

Music Basics

A pre-instrumental programme introducing children to music fundamentals through singing, movement, rhythm games, and basic notation. A fun and engaging first step into the world of music.

Ages 5–7
🎻

Violin

Learn violin in a supportive group setting. Students develop bowing technique, posture, and music reading skills while playing together with their peers.

Ages 7+
🎻

Cello

Explore the rich tones of the cello in group lessons. Cellos are available for hire ($100/year) for students who don't yet have their own instrument.

Ages 7+
🎹

Keyboard

Build keyboard skills including reading music, chord progressions, and playing melodies. A popular class — waitlists may apply.

Ages 7+
🎸

Guitar

Learn acoustic guitar basics including chords, strumming patterns, and simple songs. A very popular class — waitlists may apply.

Ages 7+
🪈

Flute

Develop breath control, tone production, and fingering technique in group flute lessons. Suited to slightly older students who can manage the instrument.

Ages 10+
🎵

Recorder

A wonderful first wind instrument. Students learn music reading, breath control, and ensemble playing through the recorder.

Ages 6.5+
🎺

Trumpet

Build embouchure, breath support, and brass playing skills. Students learn together in an encouraging group environment.

Ages 8+
🥁

Drums

Develop rhythm, coordination, and percussion skills. Learn to read rhythmic notation and play a variety of styles and patterns.

Ages 7+
🎤

Choir

Open to all Gallery students at no extra cost! Sing together in a fun, supportive group and perform at the annual concert.

Free!

Ensemble

For students with approximately 2 years of prior tuition. Play music together in a small group and develop ensemble skills.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do classes cost?

The annual contribution is $210 per class for the year. Classes are heavily subsidised by the Ministry of Education. Instrument hire is additional: violins $80/year, cellos $100/year.

When and where are classes held?

All classes are on Saturday mornings at Dyer Street School, 9 Dyer Street, Lower Hutt. Each class is 30 minutes long.

What age does my child need to be?

Most instrument classes start at age 7. Music Basics is for ages 5–7. Recorder starts at 6.5, trumpet at 8+, and flute at around 10+.

Does my child need their own instrument?

Students are expected to have access to an instrument at home and bring it to class each week. Gallery Music Centre has violins available for hire ($80/year) and cellos ($100/year). Tutors can advise on purchasing instruments.

How are class placements decided?

Placements are determined by tutors and the Gallery Coordinator. Priority goes to students advancing from our pre-instrumental programme, then to siblings of existing students and families from Dyer Street School.

What about music books and materials?

Tutor books are chosen by each tutor and are usually available from them directly or from local music stores. This is an additional cost beyond the class contribution.

Is there a waitlist?

Some classes (especially keyboard and guitar) have significant waitlists. Please contact us to be added to a waitlist.

Can my child learn more than one instrument?

Students wishing to learn more than one instrument will be placed in additional classes if spaces are available.

What is the annual concert?

Gallery holds an annual concert (usually in Term 3) where students perform for family and friends. It's a highlight of the year and a wonderful chance to celebrate the progress every student has made.

How do I enrol?

Visit our enrolment page or contact us at administration@gallerymusic.org if you have more questions.
